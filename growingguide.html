🍅 Tomato Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Well-draining, compost-rich soil, 5-gallon container minimum, Tomato cage/stake/trellis, Deep, consistent watering, Calcium-rich fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-April & August-September | Western: April-May | Midwest: Late April-May | Northeast: May-June | Pacific NW: May-June

🌿 How to Grow: Plant Deep (bury 2/3 of stem), Water Regularly (deep soak 1-2 times per week), Feed Every 2-3 Weeks, Prune Suckers (optional), Support Early (add cage/stake when planting).

🍅 When to Harvest: Fully colored and slightly soft tomatoes are ready. Pick regularly to encourage more fruit.

🌟 Pro Tip: Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before your last frost date.

🌶️ Jalapeño Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Good Soil with compost, 3-5 gallon pot if container growing, Consistent water (even moisture), Optional stake for heavy fruit, Balanced fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-April & August-September | Western: April-May | Midwest: Late April-May | Northeast: May-June | Pacific NW: May-June

🌿 How to Grow: Start indoors 8-10 weeks before frost (optional), Plant Deep (bury to first true leaves), Water evenly when top 1-2 inches are dry, Feed every 2-3 weeks, Optional support, Watch for pests.

🍴 When to Harvest: Green jalapeños are ready when firm and 3-4 inches long. Leave on plant to ripen to red for sweeter, hotter flavor.

🌟 Quick Tips: Mulch to retain moisture. Wait until soil is 60°F+ to plant. Regular picking encourages more fruit.

🥕 Carrot Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Loose, sandy soil with no rocks, 12-inch deep container minimum, Consistent moisture, Low-nitrogen fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: September-March | Western: March-June & July-August | Midwest: April-June & July-August | Northeast: April-June & July-August | Pacific NW: April-June & July-August

🌿 How to Grow: Prepare deep, loose soil, Direct sow only, Plant shallow, Water gently and consistently, Thin seedlings, Feed lightly.

🍴 When to Harvest: Ready in 60-80 days. Harvest when roots are desired size. Can be left in ground if soil isn’t frozen.

🌟 Quick Tips: Mulch helps soil stay cool, Succession plant every 2-3 weeks, Avoid compacted soil for straight carrots.

×

🌽 Sweet Corn Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Compost-rich soil, 4x4 garden space minimum, Consistent watering, High-nitrogen fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-May & July-August | Western: April-June | Midwest: April-June | Northeast: May-June | Pacific NW: May-June

🌿 How to Grow: Direct sow only, Plant in blocks not rows for pollination, Water consistently, Feed heavily, Mulch helps.

🌽 When to Harvest: Ready 60-100 days after planting. Ears are ripe when silks are brown and kernels are plump and milky.

🌟 Quick Tips: Succession plant for longer harvest, Protect seedlings from birds, Traditional “Three Sisters” planting works great.

🌿 Herb Growing Guide: Basil, Parsley, Chives & Cilantro

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Well-draining soil, Minimum 6-inch pots, Regular watering, Light fertilizer every 4-6 weeks.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-September | Western: April-August | Midwest: May-August | Northeast: May-August | Pacific NW: May-August

🌿 How to Grow: Direct sow or transplant, Water consistently, Pinch often to encourage growth.

🍴 When to Harvest: Harvest when plants are about 6 inches tall. Cut outer leaves first. Best flavor in the morning.

🌟 Quick Tips: Basil loves heat, Parsley tolerates shade, Chives regrow easily, Cilantro bolts in heat—sow regularly.

🍉 Watermelon Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Loose, compost-rich soil, Lots of space (vines can spread 6-10 feet), Consistent deep watering, High-nitrogen fertilizer early, bloom-boosting later.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-June | Western: April-June | Midwest: May-June | Northeast: May-June | Pacific NW: May-June

🌿 How to Grow: Direct sow preferred, Plant 2-3 feet apart, Water deeply, Feed regularly, Mulch to retain moisture.

🍉 When to Harvest: 70-100 days. Look for creamy yellow spot, brown tendril, hollow sound when tapped.

🌟 Quick Tips: Water less the week before harvest to boost sweetness, Smaller varieties are great for small spaces.

🎃 Pumpkin Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Rich, compost soil, Lots of space (vines can spread 10-20 feet), Deep watering, Nitrogen-rich early, bloom/fruit fertilizer later.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-July | Western: April-June | Midwest: May-June | Northeast: May-June | Pacific NW: May-June

🌿 How to Grow: Direct sow when soil is 70°F, Sow 4-5 seeds per mound, Water 1-2 inches per week, Feed regularly, Mulch to retain moisture.

🎃 When to Harvest: Deep solid color, hard skin, dried vine. Cut with stem attached.

🌟 Quick Tips: Water at base to prevent disease, Attract bees for pollination, Pumpkins are heavy feeders.

🥒 Cucumber Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Compost-rich soil, Space to sprawl or trellis, Consistent deep watering, Balanced fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-August | Western: April-July | Midwest: May-July | Northeast: May-July | Pacific NW: May-July

🌿 How to Grow: Direct sow or start indoors, Bush: 12-18 inches apart, Vine: 36-60 inches apart, Water consistently, Feed regularly, Mulch helps.

🥒 When to Harvest: Firm, green cucumbers, 6-8 inches. Pick daily. Frequent picking encourages more fruit.

🌟 Quick Tips: Trellis for cleaner cucumbers, Water at base to prevent mildew, Succession plant for more harvests.

×

🥔 Potato Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Loose, compost-rich soil, Grow bags, raised beds, or ground, Consistent deep watering, Balanced or low-nitrogen fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: January-March & August-September | Western: February-April | Midwest: March-May | Northeast: April-May | Pacific NW: February-April

🌿 How to Grow: Use seed potatoes, Plant 4 inches deep, Hill soil around stems, Water consistently, Feed lightly.

🥔 When to Harvest: New potatoes: 10 weeks, Full size: when plants die back. Cure before storing.

🌟 Quick Tips: Potatoes love loose soil, Keep developing tubers covered, Grow bags are space-saving.

🌿 Fennel Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Compost-rich soil, Garden or 12-inch pot, Regular watering, Light fertilizer every few weeks.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: September-March | Western: March-May & August-September | Midwest: April-June & August-September | Northeast: April-June & August-September | Pacific NW: April-June & August-September

🌿 How to Grow: Direct sow preferred, Water consistently, Feed lightly, Harvest before bolting.

🍴 When to Harvest: Bulbs: Tennis ball size, Leaves: Any time, Seeds: When flowers dry.

🌟 Quick Tips: Fennel bolts in heat, Mulch helps retain moisture, Attracts beneficial insects.

🍓 Strawberry Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Loose, compost-rich soil, Raised beds, containers, or hanging baskets, Regular deep watering, Berry fertilizer every 3-4 weeks.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: October-February | Western: February-April | Midwest: March-May | Northeast: March-May | Pacific NW: February-April

🌿 How to Grow: Choose type (June, Ever, Day-neutral), Space 12-18 inches, Water consistently, Mulch heavily, Feed regularly.

🍴 When to Harvest: Fully red, firm berries. Pick daily. Pinch stem to harvest.

🌟 Quick Tips: Remove runners first year, Netting may be needed, Replace plants every 3-4 years.

×

🍇 Blackberry & Raspberry Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Compost-rich soil, Space along fences or trellises, Deep regular watering, Berry fertilizer or compost each spring.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: November-March | Western: February-April | Midwest: March-May | Northeast: March-May | Pacific NW: February-April

🌿 How to Grow: Space erect varieties 2-3 ft apart, trailing 4-6 ft, Water deeply 1-2x per week, Mulch heavily, Use trellises, Feed annually.

🍇 When to Harvest: Blackberries: Fully black, plump. Raspberries: Fully colored, easily pull off. Check daily during peak.

🌟 Quick Tips: Prune dead canes, Netting may be needed, Berries spread aggressively.

🫘 Green Bean Growing Guide (Bush & Pole)

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Compost-rich soil, Bush: no support, Pole: trellis or poles, Regular deep watering, Compost or low-nitrogen fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-August | Western: April-July | Midwest: May-July | Northeast: May-July | Pacific NW: May-July

🌿 How to Grow: Direct sow only, Bush: 2-4 inches apart, Pole: 4-6 inches apart, Water consistently, Feed lightly, Mulch helps.

🫘 When to Harvest: Firm, pencil-size beans. Pick regularly for more production.

🌟 Quick Tips: Succession plant for more harvests, Pole beans last longer, Water at base to prevent leaf disease.

🧄 Garlic Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Loose, compost-rich soil, Raised beds or deep containers, Consistent even watering, Compost or low-nitrogen fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: October-December | Western: September-November | Midwest: September-November | Northeast: September-November | Pacific NW: September-November

🌿 How to Grow: Plant cloves 2 inches deep, 4-6 inches apart, Water consistently, Mulch heavily, Feed once in spring.

🧄 When to Harvest: Lower leaves brown, 4-6 green leaves remain. Cure in cool, dry place 2-4 weeks.

🌟 Quick Tips: Garlic takes 8-9 months, Hardneck types produce scapes, Rotate crops yearly.

🍍 Pineapple Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Well-draining, sandy soil, Minimum 5-gallon pot or garden bed, Light regular watering, Balanced or fruit fertilizer every 6-8 weeks.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: Year-round (March-July best) | Western: March-July | Midwest/Northeast/Pacific NW: Containers only, bring indoors in winter

🌿 How to Grow: Start from store-bought crown, Dry crown 2-3 days, Plant in soil covering base only, Water lightly, Feed every 6-8 weeks, Provide warmth (65°F-85°F).

🍍 When to Harvest: 18-24 months to produce fruit. Ready when fully colored, sweet-smelling, slightly soft to the touch.

🌟 Quick Tips: Drought-tolerant, Don’t overwater, Produces pups (baby plants) you can replant, Great for patios or sunny windows.

🐉 Dragon Fruit Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Well-draining, sandy or cactus mix soil, Large pot (15-20 gallons) or in-ground, Moderate regular watering, Cactus or low-nitrogen fertilizer every 4-6 weeks.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: March-August | Western: April-July | Midwest/Northeast/Pacific NW: Containers only, bring indoors in winter

🌿 How to Grow: Start from cutting, Needs trellis or post for support, Water lightly, Dry between watering, Feed every 4-6 weeks, Needs warmth (65°F-85°F).

🐉 When to Harvest: 6-9 months from cutting. Pick when bright pink/red and slightly soft to the touch.

🌟 Quick Tips: Blooms at night, Some varieties need hand-pollination, Grow in pots for portability.

🟢 Pea Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Well-draining compost-rich soil, Raised beds or deep containers, Trellis or stakes for climbing, Regular light watering, Light feeding with compost.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.: Southern: Late September-February | Western: January-April | Midwest: March-May | Northeast: March-May | Pacific NW: February-April

🌿 How to Grow: Direct sow only, Sow seeds 1 inch deep, 2 inches apart, Water consistently but lightly, Provide support, Avoid overfeeding nitrogen.

🟢 When to Harvest: Snap & snow peas: Pick when pods are plump but tender. Shelling peas: Pick when pods are full but not over-mature.

🌟 Quick Tips: Peas love cool weather, Mulch helps retain moisture, Succession plant every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest.
